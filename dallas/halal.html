Muslim Visitor Guide

Halal Food & Muslim Guide

Dallas has a large and well-established Muslim community — halal food is genuinely easy to find, especially in Richardson. This guide covers everything Muslim visitors need for a comfortable World Cup 2026 trip.

Key Info

Dallas for Muslim Visitors

Halal food availability
Excellent — particularly in Richardson (15 min from downtown). Multiple certified halal restaurants across all price ranges.
Mosques
Several masjids in the Dallas-Fort Worth area. Islamic Association of North Texas (IANT) in Richardson is the main community centre.
Prayer at the stadium
AT&T Stadium has multi-faith prayer rooms. Bring a small prayer mat. Security may inspect — allow extra time at the gate.
Alcohol at matches
Alcohol is sold inside AT&T Stadium. It is not forced upon you — simply decline. Non-alcoholic options are widely available at all concession stands.

Practical Tips

Muslim-Friendly Travel Tips

Verify halal certification
Always verify halal status with the restaurant directly — use Zabihah.com or the Halal Trip app to find certified halal restaurants in DFW. Don't assume a restaurant is halal based on name or cuisine type alone.
Halal grocery stores
Al-Barakah Halal Meat Market in Richardson and Halal Meat Shop on Greenville Ave stock a wide range of certified halal meat, packaged foods, and Middle Eastern groceries for self-catering.
Wudu at the stadium
AT&T Stadium bathrooms have standard sinks. Wudu-friendly facilities are not officially confirmed — plan to perform wudu at your hotel before match day. Prayer room availability at the stadium will be confirmed closer to the tournament.
Qibla direction in Dallas
From Dallas, the qibla direction is approximately 48–50° northeast. Use the Muslim Pro or Athan app for a precise compass reading at your specific location.
Alcohol is everywhere
Alcohol is served at the stadium, in restaurants, and at fan zones. It is not compulsory — simply decline when offered. Non-alcoholic alternatives are always available. The Richardson halal restaurant cluster is entirely alcohol-free.
Richardson — your base
Consider staying in Richardson if halal food access is a priority. 15 min on DART to downtown Dallas, 30 min to the stadium on match days, and surrounded by halal restaurants within walking distance.
