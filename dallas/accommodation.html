Where to Stay

Accommodation Guide

Arlington, downtown Dallas, or Fort Worth? Each has real pros and cons depending on how many matches you're attending, your budget, and what you want to do between games. Here's the honest breakdown.

The Big Decision

Arlington vs Dallas vs Fort Worth

Arlington
Walking distance to stadium
The most convenient option if you're attending multiple matches. Walk or short Uber to AT&T Stadium. Limited nightlife and restaurant options compared to Dallas, but you won't be spending money on transport.
Best for
Attending 3+ matches · Groups who want to walk to the stadium · Avoiding match-day transport chaos
Price range
$150–350/night during match days · Books out fast — reserve now
Downtown Dallas
Best all-round choice
The best balance of access and experience. DART to the stadium (TRE + shuttle), plus full access to Dallas's restaurants, fan zones, and nightlife. Uptown and Deep Ellum are both walkable.
Best for
First-time visitors · Exploring Dallas culture · Fan zones · Mix of matches and sightseeing
Price range
$120–280/night · More hotel options than Arlington
Fort Worth
Budget-friendly, charming
Often overlooked but genuinely great. 30 minutes to the stadium, much cheaper hotels, and the Fort Worth Stockyards is one of the most unique Texas experiences available. TRE train to stadium on match days.
Best for
Budget-conscious visitors · Families · Those wanting a quieter base with Texas character
Price range
$80–180/night · Significantly cheaper than Dallas on match days

Booking Advice

Book Early. Very Early.

Book now — prices will double
Dallas hotel prices during the World Cup are already 2–3× normal rates. By the time matches start, available rooms will be scarce and prices will be extreme. Book refundable rates now and cancel later if plans change.
Free cancellation is your friend
Book fully refundable rates even if they're slightly higher. Match schedules can shift, and having flexibility costs very little compared to being locked in. Booking.com and VRBO both offer strong cancellation options.
Check checkout day carefully
Many hotels require minimum stays around match days. If you're only attending one game, look for hotels with no minimum stay requirements — or book a full week even if you plan to arrive late.
Consider checking out on a non-match day
Match days have surge pricing on Uber and chaotic traffic. If possible, schedule your departure day for a quiet Tuesday or Wednesday — you'll have an easier airport journey and cheaper rideshare prices.
