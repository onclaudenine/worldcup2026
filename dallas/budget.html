Tipping Culture

Tipping in Texas

Tipping is not optional in the US. Service staff are often paid below minimum wage with the expectation that tips make up the difference. Not tipping is considered rude and can cause genuine financial hardship for the worker. Budget for it.

Save Money

Budget Smart Tips

🏛️
Dallas Museum of Art is Free
General admission is free every day. One of the best art museums in the American South — world-class collection, no charge. A great air-conditioned option on a hot afternoon.
🚆
DART saves $30–50 per day
A DART day pass is $6. The equivalent in Uber rides to the same places is $50–80. Use DART for anything downtown and save the Ubers for when there's truly no transit option.
💳
Use Wise or Revolut
Airport ATMs and currency exchange booths charge 5–8% in fees. A Wise or Revolut card gives you near-perfect exchange rates with no hidden charges. Set one up before you fly.
🌮
Food trucks for lunch
Dallas has an excellent food truck scene. Excellent tacos, BBQ, and international food for $10–14. Save the sit-down restaurants for dinner and use food trucks and markets for lunch.
🏟️
BYOW to the stadium (water)
Empty refillable water bottles are permitted through AT&T Stadium security. Fill up at water fountains inside. Stadium water costs $6–8 per bottle — a family of 4 saves $25+ per match.
🛒
Walmart / HEB for snacks
Stock up on snacks and drinks from a grocery store for your hotel room. HEB and Walmart are a fraction of the cost of convenience stores and hotel minibars.
